# **Voice & Tone Style Guide: Amen O. Olabode**

## Core Identity: "Precision meets Personality"

The writing style of this portfolio is a unique blend of high-level technical authority and relatable, authentic personality. It bridges the gap between a strict, data-driven Product Manager and a down-to-earth creator. It is confident, narrative-driven, and culturally grounded.

## 1\. The Four Pillars of Voice

### A. The Hook (Storytelling)

Treat product problems like a narrative hook. Invite the reader into the room and set the scene immediately.

* Do use: "Picture this:", "We weren't just guessing—the data was right there lol."  
* Don't use: "Users were experiencing a 15% drop-off rate due to UI constraints."

### B. The Execution (Action-Oriented)

The language is decisive and architectural. It’s about building and dismantling to solve the problem.

* Do use: "We didn't just put a fresh coat of paint on it; we tore it down." / "We pivoted to bits over atoms."  
* Don't use: "We updated the legacy system."

### C. The Impact (Grounded Narrative)

Results should be transformational but grounded in reality. Tell the story of the success without hyperbolic exaggerations. Let the achievement speak for itself.

* Do use: "The numbers told the real story..." / "Operational visibility went from zero to a hundred." / "We found immediate market fit."  
* Don't use: Exaggerations like "The impact was seismic," or dry corporate speak like "We saw a moderate increase."

### D. The Naija Flavor (Authenticity)

Break the fourth wall using Nigerian pop-culture slang and Pidgin. It rewards user interaction and creates a delightful contrast to the serious tech talk.

* The Vibe: Playful, hyping up the user, deeply authentic.  
* The Arsenal: "Osheyyy\! 🙌", "Ogaju\! 🔥", "Naso\! 💯", "Mad o\! 🤯", "You dey feel am? 😎"

## 2\. Structural Framework: The P-D-W-R Method

Every case study follows a strict, scannable, four-part story arc.

* The Problem: Set the scene and emphasize the friction.  
* Data & Research: Prove it wasn't a guess.  
* What We Did: The architecture and strategy.  
* The Result: The direct business impact.

## 3\. The PM/Dev Lexicon

The vocabulary borrows from both high-level strategy and deep-level engineering.

* Strategy: Cross-functional execution, Market fit, Untapped market, Walled garden, Trapped revenue  
* Technical: Microservices-driven, Asynchronous processing, Dynamic routing, Webhook fires

## 4\. Phrasing Rules & Idioms

* Direct Storytelling: Get straight to the point. Tell a compelling story without being long-winded or overly wordy.  
* Contrast Statements: Use juxtaposition for narrative emphasis. (e.g., "Product Manager first, Developer second", "bits over atoms").
